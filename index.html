A Brief Introduction

My name is Manuel and I am currently enrolled in my third year of Mechatronics Enginnering at McMaster University. My name is Manuel Lemos and I am applying to the 2020 Mechanical Contractors Association Hamilton Niagara Scholarship. I believe I am a strong candidate for this scholarship as I am an extremely hard working and motivated student who strives to exceed expectation. I attained a high school graduating average of 95.2% while simultaneously working a part time job and taking part in OFSAA reaching soccer and volleyball teams. Having transitioned to University my extreme dedication to learning and maintaining an active role in my school community has not been lost. I have taken up intramural volleyball and started a peer learning study group. As a result of my academic efforts I have been able to achieve a Cumulative GPA of 10.20 and attain Deans’ Honour List both years I have studied. These grades have enabled me to enter into, and excel within, the highly competitive Mechatronics Engineering Program at McMaster University. I consistently attend peer study groups and Course provided Tutorials using as much of the resources available to me as possible. As a result of these efforts I have been able to achieve a second-year average of 11.00 drastically improving upon my first year. Over the course of the last several years I have volunteered within my community in a number of different ways. At the high school I attended, I visited two upper year classrooms to talk to the students about the transition into University and provide insight into how to succeed as they progress through the sometimes-scary transition. Many high school students still are not sure exactly what they want to do and are reluctant to talk about their future’s. Telling them that it is okay to not have a specific direction and that I myself don’t know where I will end up proved effective at relaxing some of the students. Furthermore, I completed a near identical exercise for a group of senior elementary school students as they transitioned into high school.
